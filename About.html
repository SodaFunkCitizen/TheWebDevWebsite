About The Buggles

The pioneers of the video music era, forever changing the landscape of pop culture.

Who Were The Buggles?

The Buggles were a groundbreaking English new wave duo consisting of Trevor Horn and Geoff Downes. Formed in 1977, they became famous for their hit song "Video Killed the Radio Star", which became the first music video ever played on MTV.

A Legacy That Lives On

Their debut album The Age of Plastic explored themes of technology and media, influencing countless artists. Their innovative sound continues to be celebrated by musicians and fans alike. It was recorded in Sarm East Studios in London. These studio can still be visited to this day and there some posters and plaques mentioning the buggles in the studio
